Keynote Speaker

Dr. Kate Starbird

We are excited to announce Dr. Kate Starbird, Associate Professor in the Department of Human Centered Design & Engineering (HCDE), as our keynote speaker.

Dr. Starbird's research explores the intersection of technology and society,focusing on online social platforms' potential for collaboration and addressing concerns about abuse, manipulation, and trust erosion.She teaches courses in Human-Computer Interactions (HCI), Information-Communication Technologies (ICTs), and social media behavior during crises. Currently, her research is centered on combatting disinformation.

Additionally, she serves as adjunct faculty in the Paul G. Allen School of Computer Science & Engineering and the Information School, she is a data science fellow at the eScience Institute and the director of the Emerging Capacities of Mass Participation (emCOMP). Dr. Starbird is also a co-founder of the University of Washington's Center for an Informed Public.

For more information about Dr. Kate Starbird, see her page on the University of Washington Website.

Data Infrastructures and Communities for Openness and Transparency

Data openness and transparency improve knowledge production and dissemination. Yet, documenting and sharing data remains a challenge. Those practices are not routinely integrated in the data workflows; data work is not rewarded as an intellectual and academic activity, and the norms and policies around data collaborations vary from domain to domain. In this talk I will describe several projects that examine data practices in various communities and develop tools and recommendations aimed at improving data curation and supporting openness and transparency. Each project description will be structured around questions crucial for any scholarly inquiry: what was studied, how, why, and what’s next.
